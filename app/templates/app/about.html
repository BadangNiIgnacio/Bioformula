About Badang ni Ignacio

Poverty in our Bicol Region is the primary reason why the Christian Life Community (CLC) members formed a non-profit organization called Christian Life Community-Bicol Region, Incorporated (CLC-BRI). We then acquired an 11 hectares land in 2007 in Barangay San Joaquin, San Fernando, Camarines Sur to address the issue. We named it “Badang ni Ignacio” (Farm of St. Ignatius) and gradually turning it into an agricultural training center for Wellness, Livelihood and Technology. We are working assiduously to transform it into a natural farm and in time has become an Agricultural Training Institute (ATI) certified Learning Site as an Integrated-Diversified Farm. Our farmhands are NC II passers in organic vegetable production, attended trainings on cacao, rice and other livelihood programs. As of now, for reasons internal to our organization, CLC-BRI is now replaced by the name Badang ni Ignacio, Inc. (BnI,Inc).

Our farm is planted with cacao, coconut trees, pili, papayas, bananas and vegetables. We are also venturing into landscaping, plant propagation and herbals. Quite hidden for now are the many fruit trees and hardwood trees we are planting. Presently we are concentrating on making organic fertilizers, pesticides and composts. Our poultry and hog projects have been seriously affected by current diseases. We’re slowly re-starting the free-range chickens and hogs. Central and essential in our farm is our spiritual life. It is the source of our mission to lend and spend our “time, talent and treasures” among the poor, especially the farmers. Motivated by the Ignatian (spirit of St. Ignatius) fire of our recollections and retreat experiences, we gather to pray and plan the day before we work, recite the rosary after lunch, and do the Examen (examination of consciousness) in community before we end the day. This kind of stewardship, expressed in friendship, joy and hospitality, characterizes our family spirit. Assuring that our religious organization continue to grow, we have at the moment several adult and youth communities nurtured by our management team who are seasoned from 40-some years of Full membership. These communities go through stages of Emerging and Observer formation process. Presently, several activities mark our formation, among them: at least an annual 3-day Ignatian retreat, a regional convention, a World CLC Day celebration and a Christmas event. The latter is always a big celebration, especially for children, attended not only by members but also by family, friends and big hearted civic, religious, NGOs and business organizations.

OUR MISSION, VISION AND VALUES

Mindful of our small but deliberate contribution to Bicol progress,
we are guided by our Vision and Mission.

Our Mission

Bringing about God’s reign in the culture and customs of the people, especially the poor, by adapting the Spiritual Exercises in the formation of CLC and other faith communities.

Our Vision

A discerning apostolic community committed to Christ through His Church, making Him present, alive and relevant among the people through the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius.

Our Values

We uphold faith, stewardship, and empowering communities through sustainable farming and training. We foster hospitality, building a family spirit of love, unity, and shared purpose.
